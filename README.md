# AquaScope
Aquatic biomes, encompassing oceans, rivers, and lakes
are critical in maintaining the equilibrium of our planet.

However, climate change and industrialization are caus-
ing rapid transformations in these environments that sur-
pass the natural adaptation capacities of resident organ-
isms. Such changes have significant implications for agri-
culture, farming and fishing industries. Therefore, knowing

“what’s in our water” is a first step towards detecting and
solving any problem quickly. Today, scientists study these

changes by collecting data which involves heavy manual re-
search to investigate problems that are unique to each site.

We do not have cost-effective technologies that can provide
real-time insights in simple English terms our end users can
understand and act upon. AI promises to make this process

much more efficient. This research provides an in-depth analy-
sis of Google Gemini, a multi-modal AI-Model and demon-
strates that computer vision-based prediction often outper-
forms sensor-based measurements with richer analysis. The

ability to reason about aquatic health without “touching”

the water is disruptive, and can change how aquatic moni-
toring is performed around the world.
